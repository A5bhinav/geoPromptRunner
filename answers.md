1. **Keep a Sleep Diary**: Track your sleep patterns for a couple of weeks. Note the time you go to bed, how long it takes to fall asleep, how often you wake up during the night, and what time you wake up in the morning. Also, record any naps, your diet, exercise, and stress levels.

2. **Evaluate Your Sleep Environment**: Ensure your bedroom is conducive to sleep. This includes having a comfortable mattress and pillows, a cool room temperature, and minimal noise and light. Consider blackout curtains or a white noise machine if necessary.

3. **Review Your Lifestyle and Habits**:
   - **Caffeine and Alcohol**: Consuming these, especially in the afternoon or evening, can disrupt sleep.
   - **Screen Time**: Exposure to screens before bed can interfere with your ability to fall asleep due to blue light.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can improve sleep, but exercising too close to bedtime might have the opposite effect for some people.

4. **Consider Stress and Mental Health**: Stress, anxiety, and depression can significantly impact sleep. Reflect on any recent changes or ongoing stressors in your life.

5. **Assess Your Diet**: Eating large meals or spicy foods close to bedtime can cause discomfort and disrupt sleep. Try to have your last meal a few hours before bed.

6. **Look for Medical Issues**: Conditions like sleep apnea, restless legs syndrome, or chronic pain can affect sleep. If you suspect a medical issue, consult a healthcare professional.

7. **Evaluate Your Sleep Schedule**: Irregular sleep patterns can confuse your body's internal clock. Try to go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on weekends.

8. **Consider Psychological Factors**: Sometimes, worrying about not sleeping can itself cause insomnia. Try relaxation techniques such as meditation, deep breathing, or progressive muscle relaxation.

9. **Consult a Professional**: If you've tried adjusting your habits and environment without success, it might be time to see a doctor or a sleep specialist. They can help identify underlying issues and suggest treatments or therapies.

By systematically evaluating these factors, you can often identify the root causes of your sleep issues and take steps to improve your sleep quality.

## Judge calibration observations (for the gold-set labeling session)

- High-severity flags look right: stale model (Gen 3 / Ring 4 called newest;
  Ring 5 launched 2026-05-28), stale pricing ($299–$349 vs current $399), and
  missed/required-subscription errors dominate — exactly the watch-list.
- The judge **over-flags at low severity**: paraphrases of true features get
  flagged as "not in fact sheet" (e.g. battery "4–7 days" vs the sheet's
  "~8-day"), inflating flag counts. The report grade (F) is driven by this
  volume — 152 raw flags. Severity-weighting (or dropping low-severity flags
  from the grade penalty) is the obvious calibration lever to discuss.
- Run was 1 run/query (verification economics); the locked baseline should use
  3 runs/query per the methodology once Gemini is on a paid key.
