## C · Features & capabilities

- **Core (strength):** Automatic exercise detection (**50+ exercises**, barbell compounds to cable accessories), automatic rep counting, sets / rest-period tracking, **rep velocity** and velocity loss, **proximity to failure**, **time under tension**, range of motion, per-muscle volume breakdowns (maintenance/growth/overload), **Session Score**, rep cadence — **no manual logging**.
- **Beyond strength:** Heart-rate zones, **VO2 max estimation**, sleep stages (deep / light / REM), recovery scoring, overnight **HRV**, all-day activity/steps/calories, **stress** detection.
- **Sensors:** Motion (IMU — accelerometer + gyroscope) + **heart-rate (PPG)** + **temperature**. ⚠️ The live how-it-works page lists **motion, heart rate, AND temperature**; some press lists only motion + heart-rate (no temperature). Treat the live three-sensor list as current.
- **Specs:** **~7-day battery life** ("7+ days" / "lasts 7 days on a charge"); weight **~28 g**; screenless (no display, no notifications). Bluetooth / interchangeable bands.
- **App / platforms:** Has a companion mobile app. {iOS vs Android specifics left blank — not confirmed on current sources this pass. Do NOT accuracy-check platform availability.}
- **Things it is NOT / common misconceptions (false-positive guards):**
  - **Screenless** — it has no display and shows no notifications (not a smartwatch).
  - **Not shipping yet** — pre-order only.
  - Its differentiator is *automatic strength tracking*; general rivals (heart rate / calories / duration) do not auto-detect reps/sets/exercises.

## D · Positioning & competitors

- **Ideal customer:** Lifters / strength-training enthusiasts who want automatic, hands-free tracking of reps, sets, velocity, and training load, in the context of overall health/longevity.
- **Key differentiators (factual):** A wearable built specifically for **automatic strength tracking** (rep/set/exercise detection, rep velocity, proximity-to-failure, time under tension, per-muscle volume) — capabilities general wearables don't offer — combined with all-day cardio/sleep/stress/recovery. Screenless form factor.
- **Named competitors (this audit's benchmark set):** **Whoop, Garmin, Apple Watch, Oura, Vitruve.** (Note: Vitruve is a velocity-based-training device, the closest strength-specific comparison; the others are general wearables. Fort's claim is that none of them auto-detect reps/sets/exercises the way it does.)
- **Most often confused with:** General fitness wearables (Whoop / Oura / Apple Watch / Garmin). A model treating Fort as "just another HR/recovery band" is a competitor-confusion flag. Also confusable by name with unrelated "Fort" products/brands.
