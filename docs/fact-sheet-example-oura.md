# Client Fact Sheet — Oura (Example, B2C)

*Worked example of the fact sheet on a real B2C startup. Facts pulled from public sources; this is what the LLM judge would check AI answers against.*

> - Prepared by: {analyst} · **Last verified: 2026-06-02** · Verified with client? No — public sources

## A · Identity & basics

- **What it is (one line):** A smart ring that tracks sleep, activity, heart rate, and recovery, paired with a health app.
- **Category / how it should be described:** Smart ring / health-and-sleep wearable.
- **Name variants & aliases:** "Oura", "Ōura", "Oura Ring", legal entity "Ōura Health Oy".
- **Founded / HQ:** Founded 2013 in Oulu, Finland · now dual HQ San Francisco, USA + Oulu, Finland.
- **Founders / key people:** Co-founders incl. Petteri Lahtela, Kari Kivelä, Markku Koskela · CEO **Tom Hale**.
- **Company stage / size:** Private, venture-backed · reported ~**$11B** valuation (Fortune, Feb 2026).
- **Website:** ouraring.com

## B · Pricing
*(as of 2026-06-02)*

- **Billing model:** One-time hardware purchase **+ a required monthly/annual membership** for full features.
- **Hardware — Oura Ring 5 (current model):**
  - Base finishes (Silver, Black) — **$399**
  - Premium finishes (Gold, Stealth, Brushed Silver, Deep Rose) — **$499**
- **Previous model — Oura Ring 4:** was **$349**; now being cleared out below that as Ring 5 launches.
- **Membership:** **$5.99/month** or **$69.99/year**. First month free for new members.
- **Free tier?** No full free tier — without membership the ring and app still work but insights are heavily limited.
- **Free trial?** First month of membership free.

## C · Features & capabilities

- **Core features they DO have:** Sleep tracking (stages, Sleep Score), Readiness and Recovery scores, 24/7 heart rate, HRV, blood-oxygen (SpO2), body-temperature trends, activity/steps, cycle insights, guided content (meditations, sleep stories) in the app.
- **Recently shipped:** **Oura Ring 5** (announced 2026-05-28, ships 2026-06-04) — ~**40% smaller** than Ring 4, thinner/lighter redesign, updated sensors.
- **Things they explicitly do NOT do / common misconceptions:** It is **not a smartwatch** — no screen, no notifications, no on-device display. It is **not free** — the membership is required for the full product. It is **not medical-grade diagnostic** equipment.
- **Key integrations:** Apple Health, Google Health Connect, Strava, Natural Cycles; third-party apps via API.

## D · Positioning & competitors

- **Ideal customer / who it's for:** Health-conscious consumers focused on sleep, recovery, and longevity who want a discreet, screenless wearable.
- **Key differentiators (factual):** Screenless ring form factor; strong sleep/recovery software; long-standing clinical-credibility positioning; up to ~8-day battery (Ring 4 generation).
- **Named competitors:** Whoop, Ultrahuman (Ring Air / Ring Pro), Samsung Galaxy Ring (~$399), RingConn.
- **Most often confused with:** Ultrahuman and Samsung Galaxy Ring (same ring form factor); Whoop (recovery focus, but a screenless band, not a ring).

## E · Known-inaccuracy watch-list
*The high-value section — the specific things AI models are likely to get wrong right now.*

- **Wrong current model:** Most models will still say the **Ring 4** (or even Ring 3) is the newest — the **Ring 5 launched 2026-05-28**. Anything calling Ring 4 "the latest" is now stale.
- **Wrong price:** Expect stale quotes of **$349** (Ring 4) or older **$299** (Gen 3). Current Ring 5 base is **$399**.
- **Missed subscription:** Models often omit the **required $5.99/mo membership**, implying the ring is a one-time purchase. This is a frequent and material error.
- **Founding/geography:** Sometimes described as a US company; it was **founded in Finland (2013)**.
- **Acquisitions models may miss:** Oura has acquired companies (e.g., Proxy, Veri, Sparta Science) — answers may not reflect this.
