# Fort — Competitor Set & Landscape (for the demo audit)

*Locked competitor set for the Fort run, what each does, and how it relates to Fort. Competitors only need present/prominence/framing in the audit (no fact sheet, no accuracy flags) — this brief is for query design, aliasing, and the analyst layer. Researched 2026-06-13.*

**Client:** Fort — strength-training wearable (auto rep/set/exercise detection + all-day cardio/sleep/stress). Pre-launch, ships Q3 2026.

**Category for the audit:** strength-training wearable / fitness tracker for lifting.

## The locked set (5)

Chosen to span the whole landscape a strength-focused buyer actually weighs — a recovery band, watches, a recovery ring, and a precision-strength device — and the brands the engines actually name in "best wearable for lifting" answers.

| # | Brand | What it is | Strength-tracking ability | Subscription | Form factor | Aliases (for matching) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Whoop** | Screenless recovery/strain band; the most-cited wearable for lifters | **Closest rival** — "Strength Trainer" logs sets/reps and adds muscular load to strain, but you build/follow the routine manually (not fully automatic) | Yes — mandatory membership | Wrist/arm band, no screen | Whoop, WHOOP, Whoop MG, Whoop 5.0, Whoop band |
| 2 | **Garmin** | Mainstream GPS fitness watches | Strength activity **auto-counts reps, sets, rest** via motion sensors (similar mechanism to Fort, but on a watch and less specialized) | No — one-time purchase | Smartwatch (screen) | Garmin, Garmin Venu, Vivoactive, Forerunner, Fenix |
| 3 | **Apple Watch** | The default mainstream smartwatch | Generic "Strength Training" workout type — mostly heart-rate/calories, **no automatic rep counting** natively | No (optional Fitness+/3rd-party apps) | Smartwatch (screen) | Apple Watch, Apple Watch Series, Apple Watch Ultra |
| 4 | **Oura** | Sleep/recovery smart ring | **None** — recovery/sleep/HRV only, no strength or rep tracking | Yes — membership | Ring | Oura, Oura Ring |
| 5 | **Vitruve** | Velocity-based-training (VBT) device for serious lifters | **Precise strength only** — measures bar speed/velocity per rep; outperforms wrist wearables on lifts but does nothing else (no sleep/cardio) | One-time + app | Bar/cable-mounted encoder | Vitruve, Vitruve Encoder |

*Adjacent class worth noting (not in the locked set): other VBT sensors — RepOne, Perch — and subscription-free bands like Amazfit Helio Strap, Ultrahuman Ring Air. If the engines name these unprompted, they're auto-discovered competitors.*

## How Fort is positioned against each

- **vs Whoop** — Fort's sharpest comparison. Both screenless, both target lifters; Fort's claim is *fully automatic* rep/exercise detection where Whoop still needs manual routine setup. This is the head-to-head the demo should feature.
- **vs Garmin** — closest on the *auto-rep-counting* mechanism, but Garmin is a general watch with a screen; Fort is strength-specialized and screenless.
- **vs Apple Watch** — Apple is the default a casual buyer already owns; Fort's wedge is "your Apple Watch doesn't actually track your lifting."
- **vs Oura** — different product (ring, recovery-only). Fort tracks recovery/sleep *too*, so a model conflating Fort with "just a recovery tracker" is a competitor-confusion flag.
- **vs Vitruve** — the precision-strength alternative. Vitruve is more accurate on bar speed but single-purpose; Fort trades some precision for all-day, all-metrics convenience.

## Expected demo story

Fort is **pre-launch and unknown**, so the dominant finding will be **absence** — when buyers ask AI "best wearable for strength training," the engines name Whoop / Garmin / Apple / Oura and **not Fort**, and where a model does mention Fort it may invent details. The competitive leaderboard will show Whoop dominant; Fort near-zero. That's the visceral pitch to the founders: *"every buyer asking AI about strength wearables is being handed your competitors, and you're invisible."*
